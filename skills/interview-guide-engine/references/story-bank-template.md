# Story Bank — Template

The story bank lives at `job-search/story-bank.md` in the user's Cowork folder. It holds the user's 5–10 master interview stories in STAR+R form so guides reuse proven material instead of regenerating it. The user owns this file — the skill appends and updates only with their OK.

The STAR+R idea — keeping a Reflection line on every story — is adapted from [career-ops](https://github.com/santifer/career-ops) (MIT).

---

```markdown
# Interview Story Bank

_Last updated: YYYY-MM-DD_

## Story: <short memorable handle, e.g. "The migration nobody wanted">

- **Themes:** 0-to-1 build, stakeholder conflict, regulated industry
- **Situation:** One or two sentences of context — company, team, stakes.
- **Task:** What the user owned, specifically.
- **Action:** What they actually did. The decisions, not the meetings.
- **Result:** The measurable outcome. Numbers if they exist.
- **Reflection:** What they learned or would do differently — the line that makes it a senior answer.
- **Used in:** [Company] panel 2026-05, [Company] HM screen 2026-06
- **Notes:** How it landed, follow-up questions it drew, framing that worked.
```

---

## Field notes

- **Themes are the index.** Tag each story with the role themes it answers (use the same plain names the guides use: "0-to-1 build," "post-acquisition integration"). Matching happens on these.
- **Reflection is mandatory.** A story without one is a status report. If the user can't articulate the learning, help them find it before banking the story.
- **Used-in is the memory.** Knowing a story was already told at a company prevents repeating it across rounds — and a story that keeps landing well is the one to lead with.
- **Facts are the user's.** Never embellish a banked story. Adaptation means choosing which true details to emphasize for this role, nothing more.
